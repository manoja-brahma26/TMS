# Demo Use Cases for the AI-First Travel Management System

## 1. Demo Objective

The demo pack should showcase the parts of the proposed solution that best demonstrate business value, differentiation, and credibility of execution.

## 2. Recommended Demo Use Cases

### Demo 1: Conversational Trip Request Creation

**Scenario:** An employee asks for travel to a client meeting using natural language.

**What to Show**

- request capture through chat or portal
- automatic pre-fill of traveler details
- use of project, calendar, and policy context
- generation of a structured travel request

**Why It Matters**

Shows improved employee experience and the role of context-aware AI at the first step of the workflow.

### Demo 2: Intelligent Itinerary Recommendation with Policy Explanation

**Scenario:** The system recommends ranked travel options and explains which are compliant.

**What to Show**

- multiple itinerary choices
- compliant option highlighted
- explanation of why a preferred option is out of policy
- alternative recommendation with business rationale

**Why It Matters**

Shows how AI reduces effort while improving compliance and transparency.

### Demo 3: Smart Approval Routing and Auto-Approval for Low-Risk Travel

**Scenario:** A routine in-policy trip is evaluated, routed, and approved with minimal friction.

**What to Show**

- dynamic approver selection
- policy summary for manager
- auto-approval path for eligible requests
- escalation for exception cases

**Why It Matters**

Shows measurable cycle-time reduction and practical automation with controls.

### Demo 4: Agency Coordination and Booking Status Tracking

**Scenario:** An approved itinerary is sent to the travel agency and tracked to confirmation.

**What to Show**

- structured request handoff
- booking status updates
- issue or delay handling
- traveler and travel desk visibility into progress

**Why It Matters**

Shows brownfield realism and how the new platform improves the current agency-led process without breaking it.

### Demo 5: Post-Trip Expense Readiness and Anomaly Detection

**Scenario:** After travel completion, the system supports reimbursement readiness and flags unusual claims.

**What to Show**

- receipt reminders
- linkage to finance workflow
- anomaly or missing-document detection
- visibility into reimbursement readiness

**Why It Matters**

Shows that the solution covers the full travel lifecycle and creates downstream value beyond booking.

## 3. Demo Design Principles

- keep each demo tied to a business persona
- show where AI helps and where humans stay in control
- show at least one policy or audit checkpoint in each demo
- highlight brownfield integration realism
- keep the flow crisp and outcome-oriented

## 4. Recommended Demo Sequence

1. Trip request creation
2. Itinerary and policy intelligence
3. Approval decisioning
4. Agency coordination
5. Expense follow-through

This sequence tells one connected story across the full lifecycle.
