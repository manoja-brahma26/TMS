# Main Expected Deliveries in the RFP

The RFP is asking the responding team to deliver a complete proposal package, not just an idea. The main expected deliveries are:

1. A written proposal explaining the business solution, technical architecture, delivery plan, commercials, risks, team, and future vision.
2. A clear 3Cs-based architecture showing how the solution maps to `Core`, `Context`, `Coordination`, and the deterministic controls.
3. A brownfield transition plan showing how the new TMS will coexist with the current system, email approvals, travel agency, HR, finance, and policy systems.
4. A functional solution design covering travel request, itinerary planning, approvals, booking coordination, expense support, risk alerts, and reporting.
5. A data and AI design describing the travel ontology, context graph, memory, agents, orchestration, governance, and security approach.
6. A phased implementation roadmap with migration, parallel run, cutover, testing, support, and change management.
7. A commercial proposal with effort estimates, timeline, staffing, licensing, infrastructure, and pricing assumptions.
8. `4-5` demo use cases or prototypes that showcase the most important parts of the proposed solution.
9. `3 summary slides` for the finalist presentation:
   - business value proposition
   - solution differentiation
   - the most critical point the team wants the jury to remember
10. A live presentation for shortlisted teams:
   - `3 mins` proposal summary
   - `7-8 mins` demos
   - `4-5 mins` Q&A
